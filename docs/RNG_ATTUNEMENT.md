## Key Measurements

### 1. Tone Arm (0-10)

**Purpose**: Represents overall "charge" level on the subject being addressed.

**Interpretation**:
- **2-6**: Optimal range (normal, workable)
- **0-2**: Very low charge (may indicate completion or flatness)
- **6-8**: Elevated charge (resistance, material to process)
- **8-10**: Very high charge (significant material being addressed)

**Similar to**: E-meter tone arm position

### 2. Needle Position (-100 to +100)

**Purpose**: Momentary fluctuations indicating immediate energetic state.

**Interpretation**:
- **-100 to -50**: Strong negative deflection (heavy releasing)
- **-50 to -10**: Moderate negative (gentle releasing)
- **-10 to +10**: Neutral zone (minimal activity)
- **+10 to +50**: Moderate positive (building charge)
- **+50 to +100**: Strong positive (significant charge accumulation)

**Similar to**: E-meter needle dial

### 3. Needle States

#### FLOATING (Green)
- **Description**: Small, rhythmic oscillations around neutral
- **Significance**: **Release/End Phenomenon (EP)** - The most important indicator!
- **Action**: Good sign to end process or move to next item
- **Similar to**: E-meter Floating Needle (F/N)

#### RISING (Amber)
- **Description**: Consistent upward movement
- **Significance**: Building charge, resistance on item being addressed
- **Action**: Continue processing the current item
- **Similar to**: E-meter rising needle

#### FALLING (Blue)
- **Description**: Consistent downward movement
- **Significance**: Releasing charge, processing occurring
- **Action**: Continue, allow the release to complete
- **Similar to**: E-meter falling needle

#### ROCKSLAM (Red)
- **Description**: Violent, rapid oscillations
- **Significance**: Heavy charge, significant material being addressed
- **Action**: Major item found, approach carefully
- **Similar to**: E-meter rock slam (R/S)

#### THETA BOP (Purple)
- **Description**: Regular, rhythmic movement pattern
- **Significance**: Specific type of charge, often related to exteriorization
- **Action**: Note the pattern, may indicate specific phenomenon
- **Similar to**: E-meter theta bop

#### STUCK (Gray)
- **Description**: Little to no movement
- **Significance**: Neutral state or blocked/stuck condition
- **Action**: May need to change approach or item

### 4. Coherence (0-100%)

**Purpose**: Measures how organized/patterned the readings are (vs. chaotic).

**Interpretation**:
- **70-100%**: High coherence - organized, patterned, stable
- **50-70%**: Good coherence - moderate order
- **30-50%**: Fair coherence - some pattern present
- **0-30%**: Low coherence - chaotic, random

**Higher coherence** often correlates with:
- Focused attention
- Meditative states
- Strong intention
- Genuine attunement

### 5. Entropy (0-100%)

**Purpose**: Measures randomness/chaos in the readings.

**Interpretation**:
- **70-100%**: High entropy - highly random, chaotic
- **40-70%**: Moderate entropy - normal variation
- **0-40%**: Low entropy - very ordered, possibly stuck

**Note**: Moderate entropy (40-70%) is usually optimal. Too low may indicate system stuck; too high may indicate disruption.

### 6. Floating Needle Score (0-100%)

**Purpose**: Likelihood that a genuine Floating Needle (release/EP) has occurred.

**Interpretation**:
- **80-100%**: Very likely genuine F/N - strong release indicator
- **60-80%**: Probable F/N - good release
- **40-60%**: Possible F/N - minor release
- **0-40%**: Not a F/N - continue processing

**Use**: This automated score helps identify true releases vs. momentary fluctuations.

### 7. Signal Quality

#### EXCELLENT (Green)
- Clear, stable signal with high coherence
- Optimal conditions for attunement work
- Reliable readings

#### GOOD (Blue)
- Stable signal with minor fluctuations
- Good conditions for most applications
- Generally reliable

#### FAIR (Amber)
- Moderate noise present
- Usable but less reliable readings
- May need to improve focus

#### POOR (Red)
- High noise, unstable signal
- May indicate environmental interference
- Consider stopping and restarting

#### DISRUPTED (Dark Red)
- External interference or extreme fluctuations
- Rockslam or environmental disruption present
- Readings unreliable

## Usage Guide

### Starting a Session

1. **Set Baseline Tone Arm** (0-10)
   - Default: 5.0 (neutral midpoint)
   - Adjust based on intuition or initial readings
   - Typical range: 3.0-7.0

2. **Set Sensitivity** (0.1-5.0)
   - Default: 1.0 (normal)
   - **Higher** (1.5-3.0): More responsive, larger movements
   - **Lower** (0.5-0.8): More stable, smaller movements

3. **Click "Start Attunement Session"**
   - Session ID will be generated
   - System begins tracking readings

### Taking Readings

#### Manual Mode
1. Click **"Get Reading"** to get a single reading
2. Observe the needle position and state
3. Note the Floating Needle Score
4. Repeat as needed for your process

#### Auto Mode
1. Click **"Start Auto"** to begin continuous readings
2. Adjust **Refresh Rate** (100-5000ms)
   - **100-500ms**: Very fast, for rapid changes
   - **500-1000ms**: Fast, normal processing
   - **1000-2000ms**: Moderate, gentle work
   - **2000-5000ms**: Slow, deep contemplation
3. Click **"Stop Auto"** when done

### Interpreting a Session

1. **Watch for Floating Needles**
   - These indicate release/EP
   - Note when they occur in your process
   - Consider ending or moving to next item

2. **Monitor Tone Arm**
   - Should generally stay 2-6
   - If rises high (7+), significant charge present
   - If drops very low (<2), may indicate completion

3. **Observe Patterns**
   - Repeated rockslams = heavy charge item
   - Consistent rising = resistance
   - Consistent falling = releasing
   - Regular theta bops = exteriorization phenomena

4. **Check Quality**
   - EXCELLENT/GOOD: Trust the readings
   - FAIR: Usable with caution
   - POOR/DISRUPTED: Consider restarting

### Ending a Session

1. Click **"Get Summary"** to see session statistics
   - Total readings taken
   - Number of floating needles detected
   - Average values
   - Distribution of states

2. Click **"Stop Session"**
   - Marks session as inactive
   - Data preserved for review
